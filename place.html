In a Place

Reminders of days in the past– lights that haphazardly criss cross around the door, occasionally escaping the bonds of tape that constrain them. They’re a golden yellow at night but with the sun coming through the wall-length windows they’re reduced to copper wire. Paper cranes hang from the ceiling, multi-colored and multi-patterned, flying around in a circle all following one another. I sit on a wooden bench-- I imagine it oak– which hasn’t quite lost its shine but still boldly shows the signs of strain – of ours released . Silverware clinks in bowls, the bowls rattle on the countertops, the sink turns on like a sudden rainstorm, lulling the sounds of people into sounds of the things of people. I find comforn in the silence of knowing I'm not alone.
